## Class XI English — Syllabus 2026-27

## Theory: 80 Marks

## Reading 26 

## Grammar & Writing 23 

## Literature 31

### Section A — Reading Skills	(26 marks)

**1**	Unseen passage (factual / descriptive / literary) — comprehension, interpretation, inference, vocabulary. MCQ / objective.	                                                     10

**2**	Case-based factual passage with verbal/visual inputs (data, charts). Combined word limit of both passages: 600–750.	                                                                      8

**3**	Note Making — Title 1, Numbering & indenting 1, Key/glossary 1, Notes 2. (Passage ~200–250 words)	5

Summarisation (up to 50 words) — Content 2, Expression 1.	                       3

### Section B — Grammar &  Creative Writing Skills     (23 marks)

**4**–5	Grammar — Gap filling (tenses, clauses) and re-ordering / transformation of sentences. Attempt 7 of 8 questions.	                                                             7

**6** Classified Advertisement (up to 50 words), 1 of 2. Format 1 / Content 1 / Expression 1.	3

**7**	Poster (up to 50 words), 1 of 2. Format 1 / Content 1 / Expression 1.	      3

**8**	Speech (120–150 words) on a contemporary topic from verbal/visual cues, 1 of 2. Format 1 / Content 2 / Expression 2.	                                                              5

**9**	Debate (120–150 words) on a topical issue from verbal/visual inputs, 1 of 2. Format 1 / Content 2 / Expression 2.	                                                                             5

### Section C — Literature  (Hornbill & Snapshots)           (31 marks)

**10**	Poetry extract (Hornbill), 1 of 2 — comprehension, interpretation, analysis, inference, appreciation. 3 × 1.	                                                                                     3

**11**	Prose extract (Hornbill), 1 of 2 — comprehension, interpretation, analysis, evaluation, appreciation. 3 × 1.	                                                                                     3

**12**	Prose extract (Snapshots), 1 of 2 — comprehension, interpretation, analysis, inference, appreciation. 4 × 1.	                                                                    4

**13**	Short answers (Hornbill: 1 prose + 1 poetry), 2 of 4, in 40–50 words each. Inferential / critical thinking. 3 × 2.	                                                                                    6

**14**	Short answer (Snapshots), 1 of 2, in 40–50 words. Inferential / critical thinking. 3 × 1.	                                                                                                                   3

**15**	Long answer (Hornbill prose/poetry), 1 of 2, in 120–150 words. Analytical & evaluative, extrapolation beyond the text. 1 × 6.	                                           6 

**16**	Long answer (Snapshots), 1 of 2, in 120–150 words. Global comprehension, analytical & evaluative response. 1 × 6.	                                                                           6

#### Quick reminders for students

* Every writing and literature question has a choice — practise the type you answer best.
* Mind the word limits: Summary / Advertisement / Poster ≤ 50 words; Speech & Debate 120–150 words.
* Short literature answers 40–50 words; long literature answers 120–150 words.
* Books: Hornbill is the main reader (Q10, 11, 13, 15); Snapshots is the supplementary reader (Q12, 14, 16).
